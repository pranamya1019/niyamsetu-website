Exclusive Investigation

Filing Irregularities Are Costing
Indian Businesses ₹46,000 Crore A Year

Our investigation reveals that the majority of penalties paid by Indian businesses are fully avoidable. Late filings, missed deadlines, and ignored notices are bleeding companies dry, while regulators collect record amounts in fines.

₹46K Cr
Total penalties collected by CBIC, CBDT, MCA & EPFO from Indian businesses · FY2023–24
⚡ GST
GST Council Slashes Late Fee Cap; GSTR-9 Annual Return Deadline Extended to March 31
By GST Council Desk · CGST Act § 47 & Notification 07/2025
The 55th GST Council (Dec 2024) capped late fees for GSTR-9 annual returns at ₹20,000 for taxpayers with turnover up to ₹20 crore. However, GSTR-3B monthly defaults continue to attract ₹50/day (nil filers) to ₹200/day, compounding across CGST and SGST. CBIC circular 230/2025 clarifies input tax credit reversal timelines under Rule 37A.
GSTR-3B Late Fee
per day · CGST + SGST
₹200
GST portal's new IMS (Invoice Management System), effective Oct 2024, auto-flags mismatches between GSTR-2B and purchase registers. Reconciliation failures now trigger automated notices under Section 61.
Source: CBIC Circular 230/2025 · 55th GST Council Minutes
🏛 MCA
MCA3 Portal Overhaul: AOC-4 & MGT-7 Deadlines Tightened; ₹100/Day Penalties Enforced
By Corporate Affairs Desk · Companies Act 2013 § 92, 137, 403
MCA's V3 portal (Phase 3, Jan 2025) now applies real-time penalty calculators for late AOC-4 (financial statements) and MGT-7 (annual return) filings. A 30-day delay on both forms costs ₹6,000 in additional fees alone. The Ministry has begun attaching DINs of repeat defaulters.
ROC Filing Penalty
per document per day
₹100
New CFSS-2025 (Companies Fresh Start Scheme) discussions are underway for SME relief. Meanwhile, Section 447 fraud provisions are being invoked against directors of shell companies with persistent non-filing — carrying imprisonment up to 10 years.
Source: MCA Circular G.S.R. 803(E) · MCA V3 Advisory Jan 2025
📊 Income Tax
Budget 2025: TDS Rates Rationalised; Section 194Q Threshold Raised to ₹75 Lakh
By Tax Desk · Finance Act 2025 · IT Act § 194Q, 234E, 271H
Finance Act 2025 rationalised 5 TDS rates — Section 194D (insurance) cut from 5% to 2%, Section 194H (commission) from 5% to 2%. Section 194Q purchase TDS threshold raised to ₹75 lakh, giving relief to mid-size buyers. However, Section 271H penalties for late TDS returns remain unchanged.
Section 271H Penalty
late TDS / TCS return
₹1L
The new Form 12BAA requires employees to disclose all TDS deducted by other parties to their primary employer — simplifying year-end reconciliation but adding a new compliance touchpoint effective FY 2025-26.
Source: Finance Act 2025 · CBDT Circular 06/2025
👷 EPF / ESIC
EPFO Activates Auto-Claim Settlement; Late PF Deposits Attract 12% Interest + 25% Damages
By Labour Desk · EPF Act 1952 § 14B · ESI Act 1948 § 85B
EPFO's centralised penalty recovery system now sends automated notices within 15 days of ECR default. A 60-day delay on PF contributions triggers 17% total outflow: 12% interest + 5% damages. Beyond 6 months, damages escalate to 25% of arrears.
PF Damages (6+ months)
on delayed contributions
25%
ESIC expanded coverage to establishments with 10+ employees in 744 new districts (Jan 2025). Companies newly brought under ESIC have 3 months to register; default attracts 12% simple interest and up to ₹5,000/day penalties under the ESI Act.
Source: EPFO Circular P-11/12/35/I.C./2024 · ESIC Notification Jan 2025
🏦 RBI / FEMA
RBI Levies ₹2,900 Cr Penalties in FY24; FEMA Compounding Orders Hit Record High
By Banking & FX Desk · FEMA 1999 · RBI Act § 46 · Master Direction 2025
RBI's enforcement action in FY2023-24 covered 47 banks and NBFCs. For businesses, FEMA violations — especially delayed FC-GPR / FC-TRS filings for FDI and FPI transactions — are facing compounding penalties of up to 3× the defaulted amount or ₹2 lakh, whichever is higher.
FEMA Compounding Max
on FDI / ODI defaults
3×
RBI's Master Direction on KYC (Feb 2025 update) now mandates re-KYC for all business accounts every 2 years. NBFCs with assets above ₹500 Cr must file quarterly NBS-9 returns — first non-compliance notice period reduced from 90 to 30 days.
Source: RBI Enforcement Report FY2023-24 · RBI MD-KYC/2025
📈 SEBI
SEBI's BRSR Core Mandatory from FY25; Non-Compliance Penalties Up to ₹25 Crore
By Markets Desk · SEBI LODR 2015 · Circular SEBI/HO/CFD/2024
SEBI made BRSR Core (Business Responsibility & Sustainability Reporting) mandatory for the top 150 listed entities from FY 2024-25, expanding to top 1,000 companies by FY 2026-27. Third-party assurance of BRSR Core is now required, adding a new compliance layer for public companies.
LODR Disclosure Lapse
per stock exchange per day
₹1,000
Separately, SEBI's new cybersecurity framework (SEBI/HO/ITD/2024) mandates quarterly VAPT audits and 6-hour incident reporting for all market intermediaries — SEBI-registered stock brokers, mutual funds, and depositories face penalties up to ₹25 crore for non-compliance.
Source: SEBI LODR Amendment 2024 · SEBI/HO/CFD/CIR/P/2024/0000000062
⚖️ Labour Laws
4 Labour Codes Pending; States Begin Notifying Rules — Contract Workers & Gig Economy in Crosshairs
By Labour Desk · Code on Wages 2019 · OSH Code 2020 · IR Code 2020
While the 4 Labour Codes (Wages, Industrial Relations, Social Security, OSH) await Central enforcement notification, 13 states have now published draft rules. Businesses in Karnataka, Telangana, and Rajasthan face compliance obligations under state-level implementations. The Code on Wages mandates weekly wages for daily-rated workers in notified industries — non-compliance attracts ₹50,000 first offence rising to ₹3 lakh for repeat violations.
Wages Code Penalty
repeat violation
₹3L
Professional Tax (state levy): Maharashtra hiked PT slab from ₹200 to ₹300/month for salaries above ₹20,000, effective April 2025. Employers failing to deduct & remit PT face interest at 1.25%/month plus ₹500/day penalty under the Maharashtra PT Act.
Source: Ministry of Labour · Karnataka Shops & Establishments (Amendment) 2025
🌿 FSSAI / PCB
FSSAI Annual Return Deadline March 31; EPR Plastic Compliance Portal Now Mandatory
By Regulatory Desk · FSS Act 2006 · E-Waste Rules 2022 · Plastic Waste Mgmt Rules 2024
FSSAI's annual return (Form D-1) is due March 31 for all licensed food businesses. Non-filing attracts ₹100/day penalty and license suspension risk. The regulator cancelled 23,000+ licenses in FY24 for non-renewal — operators must renew at least 30 days before expiry to avoid gap penalties.
FSSAI Non-filing
per day of delay
₹100
MoEFCC's EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) portal now mandates annual plastic waste return filing for all producers, importers, and brand owners — including D2C e-commerce brands. First-year defaulters face ₹10,000 EPR certificate penalties; habitual defaults attract ₹25 lakh under Environment Protection Act.
Source: FSSAI Order No. SRM/FSSAI/AR/2024 · MoEFCC EPR Portal Circular 2025
